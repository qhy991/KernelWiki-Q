## Key Lessons

- For RMS norm with hidden_size=4096, 256 threads outperforms 128 threads despite lower theoretical occupancy
- Higher occupancy does not always mean better performance - memory bandwidth utilization matters more
- The sweet spot for RMS norm reduction is 256 threads (8 warps) with 4 blocks/SM minimum
- rsqrtf() + float4 vectorization + #pragma unroll 4 + __ldg() is the winning combination at 268.5% speedup
- Never include cudaDeviceSynchronize in extern C entry - it measures sync overhead not kernel time

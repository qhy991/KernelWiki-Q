## Challenge

FlashAttention MMA kernel with kStage=1 serializes K/V gmem-to-smem transfers: each K tile is loaded with a blocking CP_ASYNC_WAIT_GROUP(0)+syncthreads before Q@K^T compute, then V is loaded only after Q@K^T completes, adding full gmem latency for both K and V per loop iteration and stalling the SM pipeline


## Solution

Upgrade to kStage=2 double-buffered async prefetch: K occupies smem slot 0 and V occupies smem slot 1 (requiring 2x K/V smem), V async copy is issued to slot 1 without blocking before Q@K^T compute begins, overlapping the V gmem-to-smem transfer with Q@K^T MMA operations; after P@V, next K tile is prefetched into slot 0 while slot 1 is consumed, using CP_ASYNC_WAIT_GROUP(1) to only wait for V while next K continues in flight

## Key Lessons

- Double-buffering K/V smem with kStage=2 overlaps V gmem-to-smem async transfer behind Q@K^T compute by assigning K and V to separate smem slots (slot 0 and slot 1), hiding gmem latency — applies whenever smem budget allows 2x the K/V tile size and compute per tile is sufficient to cover one async copy
- Use CP_ASYNC_WAIT_GROUP(N) with N>0 to wait only for specific async groups while allowing later groups to continue in flight, enabling fine-grained pipeline control: WAIT_GROUP(1) waits V but lets next K copy proceed
- Prefetch the next K tile into slot 0 immediately after P@V consumes V from slot 1, then wait with WAIT_GROUP(0) at the top of the next iteration — this creates a three-phase pipeline (V-async || Q@K^T || next-K-prefetch) that keeps both smem slots and the copy engine busy

## Solution

Add a conjunction bounds check ib0 + ir * BPW / QR < bpr to the #pragma unroll loop condition to guard against out-of-bounds block indices when bpr is not aligned to BPW, at the cost of one extra integer comparison per iteration

## Key Lessons

- Adding a bounds check to a #pragma unroll loop is safe for correctness without measurable performance impact when the compiler can recognize the always-true case for aligned dimensions — the overhead of one integer comparison per iteration is negligible relative to shared-memory loads and float multiply-accumulates
- Bounds checks on block-index arithmetic are necessary for correctness when the row length (bpr) may not be a multiple of the warp-level block stride (BPW), even if the aligned case always evaluates to true

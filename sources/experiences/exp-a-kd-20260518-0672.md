## Key Lessons

- The local copy then becomes an unnecessary register-to-register move of 32 bytes, adding latency that the before version avoids by letting the compiler fuse the load directly into the inline expansion.
- This regression suggests that the local-copy pattern is context-dependent: it helps when NVCC scalarizes loads in specific calling contexts, but adds overhead when the compiler already generates wide loads through inlining.

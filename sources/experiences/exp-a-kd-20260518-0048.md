## Challenge

## Solution

Replace per-access coordinate recomputation with precomputed bitmask validity masks computed once on the host, so each kernel invocation checks bounds via a single bitwise AND and shift per dimension instead of integer multiply-add and comparison

## Results

- **speedup**: 1.001x (neutral)


## Key Lessons

- Precomputed bitmask validity masks replace per-access integer multiply-add and comparison with a single bitwise AND+shift per dimension, but the speedup is negligible for small filter sizes (T=R=S=3, only 27 positions) because the compiler already optimizes the simple recompute loop effectively and the instruction count difference is hidden by GPU scheduling overheads
- Bitmask precomputation trades host-side construction overhead and kernel register pressure (storing mask_t, mask_r, mask_s) for reduced per-iteration arithmetic; this tradeoff favors larger filter sizes where the amortized per-position savings exceed the fixed overhead
- Neutral benchmark results on small problem sizes do not invalidate an optimization technique — the bitmask approach may still benefit larger convolution parameters or scenarios where the mask is reused across many kernel launches

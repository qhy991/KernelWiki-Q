## Solution

Broadcast the scalar zero-point to half2 width with __half2half2, then apply __hsub2 to both frag_b elements, processing two half values per vector instruction and reducing the explicit instruction count from 4 scalar __hsub to 2 vector __hsub2 plus 1 broadcast

## Key Lessons

- Explicit __hsub2 vectorization is semantically correct and reduces source-level instruction count, but nvcc -O3 can auto-vectorize consecutive scalar __hsub on half2 fields into equivalent __hsub2 — so in isolation the speedup may be neutral
- Relying on compiler auto-vectorization is fragile: in more complex kernels with intervening instructions or control flow, the compiler may fail to vectorize, making explicit __half2half2 + __hsub2 the safer pattern
- Use __half2half2(val) to broadcast a scalar __half into both lanes of a half2 before a vector arithmetic operation — this is the canonical broadcast intrinsic and communicates SIMD intent to both the compiler and future readers

## Challenge

Page-based memory transfer issues 128 individual cudaMemcpy calls of 8KB each, where each synchronous call incurs ~2-3us driver dispatch and synchronization overhead that dominates the actual data transfer time on the GPU internal bus


## Solution

Scan the index arrays for consecutive runs where both src_diff==1 and dst_diff==1, merging each run of 64 consecutive pages into a single cudaMemcpy of 512KB, reducing 128 dispatch calls to just 2 and achieving better HBM bandwidth utilization through contiguous transfers

## Key Lessons

- Merge consecutive page copies into a single cudaMemcpy by scanning for runs where both src and dst indices are sequential (diff==1) — eliminates per-call driver dispatch overhead (~2-3us each) which dominates small transfers
- Contiguous bulk transfers achieve significantly better HBM bandwidth utilization than fragmented small transfers, compounding the benefit beyond just reducing dispatch count
- Run-length merging is safe when both source and destination strides are 1 — the merged transfer is byte-equivalent to the sequence of individual transfers

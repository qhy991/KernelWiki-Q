## Solution

Shrink TileShape M from 128 to 32 to exactly match the problem dimension M=32, achieving 100% warp M-utilization; reduce tile N from 128 to 64 to keep tile occupancy manageable; increase tile K from 64 to 128 to maintain pipeline depth and hide memory latency

## Key Lessons

- Match TileShape M dimension to the actual problem M when M is small (e.g. M<=64) — oversized tile M causes warps to compute padding elements that are discarded, halving effective utilization
- When shrinking tile M and N to match small problem dimensions, compensate by increasing tile K to maintain pipeline depth and amortize shared-memory load overhead across more useful MAC operations
- TileShape tuning for small-batch GEMM (M<<N) should prioritize M-exactness and K-depth over symmetrically large tiles — the 2.13x speedup here comes entirely from eliminating wasted M-padding while keeping the pipeline productive through deeper K staging

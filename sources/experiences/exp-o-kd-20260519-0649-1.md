## Challenge

Blelloch exclusive prefix scan uses O(log n) iterations in both up-sweep and down-sweep phases, each requiring a __syncthreads() barrier — for scan_size=256 this produces ~17 full-CTA barriers where all 1024 threads must synchronize, and barrier latency dominates the scan computation


## Solution

Replace Blelloch's two-phase scan with a two-level warp-shuffle exclusive prefix sum: each warp computes an intra-warp scan via __shfl_up_sync (hardware-synchronized, no __syncthreads needed), then warp 0 aggregates the per-warp partial sums with a second warp shuffle scan, reducing total __syncthreads from ~17 to 6

## Key Lessons

- Replace Blelloch's two-phase O(log n) scan with a two-level warp-shuffle scan to eliminate most __syncthreads() barriers — warp shuffle (__shfl_up_sync) is hardware-synchronized within a warp and completes without stalling other warps, applicable whenever scan_size fits within a single CTA and elements can be partitioned across warps
- Warp-level exclusive scan via __shfl_up_sync with conditional accumulation (add only when lane_id >= offset) plus subtracting the original value produces the exclusive prefix sum without needing a separate down-sweep phase — simpler and fewer barriers than Blelloch
- Two-level scan decomposition (intra-warp scan → aggregate warp sums in warp 0 → add warp offsets) scales to scan sizes much larger than WARP_SIZE while keeping __syncthreads count constant at O(num_warp_levels) rather than O(log scan_size)

## Solution

Fuse renormalization into the moeTopK kernel by accumulating row_sum_for_renormalize during the top-K selection loop (threadIdx.x==0 adds each winning weight) and dividing in-place after the loop, eliminating the separate renormalize_kernel launch and its global memory read-write pass

## Key Lessons

- Fuse lightweight post-processing (renormalization, scaling) into the producing kernel when the computation fits in registers and the extra data is already available — eliminates a full kernel launch and global memory round-trip
- Accumulating a running sum during top-K selection and applying a single inverse multiply after the loop avoids a separate reduction pass over the output array, turning O(2*topk) global reads/writes per row into O(topk) cache-friendly re-reads of just-written data

## Solution

Replace exp2f(x) with fast_pow2 that constructs IEEE 754 float via integer bit-shift ((x+127)<<23 reinterpreted as float), and replace log2f+ceilf with fast_log2_ceil that extracts the exponent field from the float bit representation and checks for nonzero mantissa to implement ceiling — both are integer-only bit operations executing in 2-3 instructions each

## Key Lessons

- Replace exp2f(x) with integer bit-shift ((x+127)<<23 reinterpreted as float) for power-of-2 computation — avoids expensive GPU FP math microcode and executes in 2-3 integer instructions; applies whenever computing 2^n for known integer n
- Replace log2f + ceilf with direct extraction of IEEE 754 exponent bits ((bits>>23) & 0xff - 127) plus mantissa nonzero check for ceiling — eliminates two FP math calls with pure integer bit manipulation; applies to any log2-ceil of positive floats
- When quantization scales are constrained to power-of-2 values (e.g. UE8M0 format), IEEE 754 bit manipulation is fundamentally faster than general-purpose math library functions because the exponent field directly encodes log2

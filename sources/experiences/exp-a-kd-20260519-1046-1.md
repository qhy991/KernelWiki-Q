## Challenge

GPTQ quantized dot product using half2 __hfma2 accumulation risks FP16 overflow when unscaled weights span [-128,127], since the FP16 dynamic range is too limited for large-magnitude intermediate products accumulated across 4 half2 pairs


## Solution

Decompose each half2 multiply-add into individual FP32 fma operations to widen accumulation dynamic range: extract halves via __low2float/__high2float, accumulate in a float register, scale in FP32, then convert back to half — avoiding half2 overflow but at the cost of doubling instruction count (8 fma vs 4 __hfma2) and adding conversion overhead

## Key Lessons

- Replacing 4 __hfma2 instructions with 8 scalar FP32 fma operations doubles the multiply-add instruction count and adds __low2float/__high2float/__half2float conversion overhead — this is a significant throughput regression (~43%) that is not compensated by the wider dynamic range in typical-case inputs
- FP32 intermediate accumulation is a correctness safeguard against worst-case FP16 overflow, but the float-to-half truncation (__float2half_rn) at every iteration boundary can actually increase rounding error compared to pure half2 __hfma2 accumulation when the overflow does not materialize
- Benchmark with representative scale factors before committing to FP32 accumulation: if scale factors keep intermediate products within FP16 range, the native __hfma2 path is both faster and more accurate due to fewer round-trip conversions

## Key Lessons

- The f16x8 kernel regresses because reducing threads from 1024 to 128 per block significantly decreases occupancy.
- The 8x reduction in threads removes the latency-hiding depth that was available in the scalar version with 32 warps per block, and the wider per-thread access pattern cannot compensate for the lost scheduling flexibility.

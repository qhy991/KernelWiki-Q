## Solution

Replace at::zeros with at::empty to skip the eager zero-fill, relying on the kernel to overwrite the final output completely during computation

## Key Lessons

- Use at::empty instead of at::zeros for output tensors that the kernel will fully overwrite — the zero-fill is wasted work that adds a full output-sized memset to every launch
- Eager zero-initialization is only necessary when partial writes are possible or when downstream consumers expect initialized padding; skip it when the producer covers the entire buffer

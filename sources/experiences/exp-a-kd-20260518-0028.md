## Solution

Cap split_kv at sm_count/2 when split_wave_aware exceeds 1, preventing excessive split counts that would overwhelm SM scheduling and cause atomic accumulation contention

## Key Lessons

- Capping split_kv at sm_count/2 limits kernel launch overhead and atomic accumulation contention when fused reduction is active — applies whenever split-wave-aware heuristics can grow unbounded with large K dimensions
- Heuristic caps must be validated on workloads where the cap actually triggers (split_wave_aware > sm_count/2); on GPUs where the natural split count is below the cap, the change is a no-op and produces neutral results
- Split-K attention performance depends on balancing parallelism (more splits) against atomic contention (more accumulators) and launch overhead (more kernels) — sm_count/2 is a reasonable upper bound

## Challenge

CausalMask<false> incorrectly applies offset_q = N_K - N_Q = 3840 to every Q-block's trip-count ceiling, forcing all 4 Q-blocks to visit nearly all 64 K-blocks (250 total trips) even though Q begins at position 0 and should only need 10 total trips


## Solution

Switch to CausalMask<true> (IsQBegin=true) which omits the offset_q term from the trip-count formula, correctly reflecting that Q starts at the beginning of the sequence so each Q-block only needs ceil_div((blk+1)*tile_q, tile_k) K-blocks

## Key Lessons

- When Q starts at the beginning of the KV sequence (IsQBegin=true), the CausalMask trip count must omit the offset_q = N_K - N_Q term — including it causes every Q-block to over-visit K-tiles by a factor proportional to the KV-sequence overhang
- Selecting the correct causal mask variant (CausalMask<true> vs CausalMask<false>) based on whether Q is at the beginning of the sequence can reduce total tile visits from O(N_K/tile_k * N_Q/tile_q) to O(N_Q/tile_q^2), yielding orders-of-magnitude speedup in asymmetric QK-length scenarios

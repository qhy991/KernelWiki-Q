## Challenge

For Float4_ (only 2 float2 pairs), the naive approach computes mul(a.x,b.x) and mul(a.y,b.y) in parallel producing two float2 intermediates p0 and p1, then add(p0,p1) combines them. The FMA approach computes mul(a.x,b.x) into a single accumulator, then fma(a.y,b.y,acc) chains into it. W


## Solution

ith only 2 pairs, the benefit is minimal: the naive version has 4 FP32 muls + 2 FP32 adds + 1 scalar add = 7 ops with 2 independent mul threads enabling intra-thread ILP, while the FMA version has 2 FP32 muls + 2 FP32 fmas + 1 scalar add = 5 equivalent ops but a serial dependency chain.

## Results

- **speedup**: 0.999x (neutral)


## Key Lessons

- Additionally, nvcc -O3 may auto-fuse the mul+add pattern in the naive version into implicit FMAs, erasing the instruction-level difference.

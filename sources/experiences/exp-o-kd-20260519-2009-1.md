## Solution

Swap A and B matrices (swap_ab) to reinterpret the problem as (N,K)×(K,M) = (1024,1024)×(1024,8), placing the large N=1024 dimension in the tile's M-direction where it aligns perfectly with the 128-wide tile (zero waste), while the small M=8 now pads to only 16 in the N-direction, reducing padded workload from 64×1024×1024 to 1024×16×1024 (4× reduction)

## Key Lessons

- swap_ab (transposing the GEMM by swapping A/B) places the large matrix dimension in the tile's M-direction to eliminate padding waste when M≪N — applies to any tiled GEMM where one dimension is much smaller than the tile size
- Padding waste scales with tile alignment: padding M=8 to 64 wastes 87.5% compute vs padding to only 16 after swap — choose the dimension ordering that minimizes total padded volume (product of all padded dimensions)

## Solution

Replace two separate cvt.rn.f16.f32 instructions with a single cvt.rn.f16x2.f32 packed instruction that converts both float lanes to half2 in one operation, reducing instruction count from 2 to 1

## Key Lessons

- Replacing two scalar cvt.rn.f16.f32 with one packed cvt.rn.f16x2.f32 reduces instruction count but may show neutral performance when the conversion is a small fraction of total loop work — the dominant latency comes from surrounding float arithmetic and the full round-trip conversion chain
- In mixed float/half2 pipelines where conversion overhead is diluted by multiply-add and unpack operations, micro-optimizing the pack instruction alone is insufficient to produce measurable gains — consider optimizing the entire conversion round-trip holistically

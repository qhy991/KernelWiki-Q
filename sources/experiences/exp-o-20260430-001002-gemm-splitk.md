When K dimension is much larger than M and N, parallelize across K slices to improve GPU utilization

## Challenge

Standard tiled GEMM parallelizes over M and N dimensions. When K >> M,N (e.g. down_proj shape M=968, N=2048, K=16384), the number of CTAs is limited by M*N tiles, leaving SMs underutilized while each CTA does excessive work along K.

## Solution

Split-K parallelization divides K dimension across multiple CTA groups

Use CUTLASS Split-K GEMM variant that partitions K into split_k_slices independent reductions. Each slice computes a partial result, and a final reduction combines them. Configuration: CTA tile 64x128x64, Warp tile 32x64x64, 3 stages, split_k_slices=4 for K>=16384.

## Key Lessons

- Split-K parallelization is effective when K >> M*N (e.g. K=16384, M=968, N=2048) to increase CTA count and SM utilization
- Split-K introduces a reduction step overhead; not beneficial when M*N already generates enough CTAs to fill all SMs
- CUTLASS Split-K uses separate reduction kernel, so the benefit depends on the ratio of compute time saved vs reduction overhead
- Tactic 7 in mizar-kernel is the split-K variant; use it for large-K shapes but benchmark against cuBLAS
- Split-K slice count should be a power of 2 and roughly equal to number of SMs for best load balancing

## Challenge

FlashAttention MMA kernel stores the O accumulator in FP16 registers (2 uint32_t per element), requiring repeated __half2float and __float2half_rn conversions at every online softmax rescale step across K/V tiles, incurring conversion instruction overhead and ~3-4 decimal digits of cumulative precision loss per rescale iteration


## Solution

Switch O accumulator storage from FP16 to FP32 (kOStorageAccFloat32=1) by storing intermediate values in float registers (4 uint32_t per element via float* reinterpretation), enabling the online softmax rescale to operate entirely in float via __fmaf_rn without intermediate half conversions, preserving full precision through all rescale iterations across K/V tiles

## Key Lessons

- Store attention O accumulators in FP32 rather than FP16 when performing online softmax rescaling across multiple K/V tiles — eliminating per-step half↔float conversions saves instruction overhead and prevents cumulative precision loss of ~3-4 decimal digits per rescale step
- FP32 accumulator storage doubles register pressure (4 uint32_t vs 2 uint32_t per element) but enables the compiler to generate more efficient FMA chains when both accumulator and rescale factor are float, often netting positive performance despite higher register usage

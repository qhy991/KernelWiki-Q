## Challenge

Residual addition and RMS normalization are implemented as two separate CUDA kernels (residual_add_kernel + rms_norm_kernel), requiring two full global-memory passes over the data and incurring kernel launch overhead twice per layer


## Solution

Fuse residual_add_kernel and rms_norm_kernel into a single fused_add_rms_norm_kernel that performs the residual add and variance accumulation in one loop, then reads the L2-cache-hot residual in a second loop for normalization, eliminating one kernel launch and one full global-memory round-trip

## Key Lessons

- Fusing sequential kernels that share data into a single kernel eliminates kernel launch overhead and keeps intermediate results L2-cache hot, avoiding redundant global-memory reads — especially effective when the intermediate data per token (hidden_size × sizeof(scalar)) fits within or near L2 cache capacity
- Kernel fusion speedup decreases slightly as per-kernel workload grows (1.655x at hidden_size=4096 vs 1.611x at hidden_size=8192) because the fixed launch-overhead savings are amortized over longer kernel execution time, but the global-memory traffic savings remain dominant

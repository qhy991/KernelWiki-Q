## Solution

Replace the host-device round-trip with a single GPU kernel launch that processes data entirely on-device, eliminating two synchronous cudaMemcpy calls and their associated synchronization barriers

## Key Lessons

- Eliminating synchronous host-device round-trips by moving processing to the GPU yields orders-of-magnitude speedup even for small data sizes, because the fixed synchronization overhead of cudaMemcpy dominates over actual transfer time
- When GPU data must be transformed by a simple lookup or arithmetic pattern, implement it as a GPU kernel rather than copying to CPU — a single kernel launch barrier is far cheaper than two synchronous memcpy barriers

## Challenge

GELU kernel computes erff(scalar / sqrt(2)) using a single-precision division instruction, which maps to the MUFU.DIV SFU with roughly 1/16th the throughput of a regular FFMA, creating a pipeline bottleneck in the per-element computation


## Solution

Replace scalar / sqrt(2) with scalar * (1/sqrt(2)), precomputing the reciprocal as HALF_ROOT_TWO_F = 0.70710678f, converting the low-throughput SFU division into a full-throughput FFMA multiply that the compiler can fuse into the surrounding 0.5f * scalar * ... FMA chain

## Key Lessons

- Replace division by a constant with multiplication by its precomputed reciprocal — division maps to the low-throughput MUFU.DIV SFU (~1/16 FFMA rate on Ampere), while multiplication is a full-throughput FFMA that the compiler can fuse into adjacent FMA chains
- Precomputing reciprocals of mathematical constants (1/sqrt(2), 1/pi, etc.) as compile-time floats is a low-risk, broadly applicable micro-optimization for any kernel where a transcendental function's argument involves a constant division

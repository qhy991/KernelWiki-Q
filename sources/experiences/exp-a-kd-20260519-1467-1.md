## Challenge

CUTLASS GEMM with TileShape<128,128,64> and WarpShape<64,64,64> processes M=16 problems with 75% wasted warp-level work in the M dimension, since each warp covers 64 M elements but only 16 are valid — padding overhead dominates compute throughput


## Solution

Reduce tile M to 16 to exactly match the problem dimension (TileShape<16,64,128>, WarpShape<16,64,64>), eliminating all padding waste in M while deepening the K pipeline to 128 to maintain throughput — achieving 100% warp M utilization instead of 25%

## Key Lessons

- Match TileShape M dimension to the actual problem M when M is small (e.g. M=16) — oversized tile M causes warp-level padding waste that dwarfs compute, since warps process more M elements than exist in the problem
- Deepen the K tile dimension (64→128) to compensate for reduced M/N tile coverage — the longer K pipeline sustains memory throughput even when M is constrained to match a small problem size
- For skinny/batched GEMM with small M, prefer narrow-M wide-N tile shapes (e.g. <16,64,128>) over square tiles (e.g. <128,128,64>) to avoid wasted SIMD lanes in the M dimension

## Solution

Adapt TileShape to 32x64x128 with WarpShape 32x64x64 to match M=32 exactly, eliminating wasted M-dimension compute, doubling N-dimension threadblock parallelism (2048/64=32 vs 2048/128=16), and improving pipeline utilization through a doubled K tile (128 vs 64)

## Key Lessons

- Match CUTLASS TileShape.M exactly to the batch M dimension to avoid wasted compute — when TileShape.M=128 but only M=32 rows are valid, 75% of tensor core cycles produce discarded results
- Smaller N-dimension tile sizes increase threadblock count along N, improving SM occupancy and parallelism for small-M large-N GEMM shapes — 2048/64=32 threadblocks vs 2048/128=16 doubles scheduling flexibility
- Increasing K tile size (128 vs 64) when reducing M tile improves software pipeline utilization by amortizing load latency over more compute iterations, partially compensating for the smaller M tile

## Solution

Switch the output accumulator R_D from FP16 storage (2 uint32_t registers per element) to FP32 storage (4 uint32_t registers per element), enabling direct __fmaf_rn FMA on float values during online rescaling without any half<->float conversion, and deferring the single float-to-half conversion to the final normalization step only

## Key Lessons

- Store MMA output accumulators in FP32 instead of FP16 during iterative online rescaling (softmax attention, running sums) — eliminates 2 conversion instructions per accumulator element per iteration, and the savings compound linearly with the number of iterations
- Deferring the single float-to-half conversion to the final normalization step converts once-per-output-element instead of once-per-tile-iteration, reducing total __float2half_rn calls by (num_K_tiles - 1)x
- For FP16 HMMA (m16n8k16) kernels, FP32 accumulator storage doubles register usage (4 vs 2 uint32_t per element) but is affordable when kWarpTileHeadDimV is small relative to available registers — profile register pressure before applying to larger head dimensions or tile configurations

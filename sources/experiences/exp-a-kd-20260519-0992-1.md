## Challenge

Two separate kernels (QKNorm RMSNorm+weight and NeoX RoPE) communicate through a ~16MB intermediate global-memory buffer, forcing a full global-memory round-trip write-then-read between the normalization and rotary embedding phases for every token-head pair


## Solution

Fuse QKNorm (RMSNorm+weight) and NeoX RoPE into a single kernel, keeping normalized+weighted values in the register array e[EPT] after the RMSNorm phase and applying __shfl_xor-based rotary embedding directly on those register-held values, eliminating the ~16MB intermediate global-memory buffer entirely

## Key Lessons

- Fuse sequential kernels that communicate through a global-memory intermediate buffer into a single kernel keeping intermediate results in registers — eliminates one full write+read round-trip and applies strongly when the intermediate buffer is large relative to compute
- Register arrays (e[EPT]) persist across computation phases within a single kernel — use them as inter-phase communication channels instead of global memory when per-thread data is small enough to fit in registers
- The __shfl_xor overhead for inter-lane data exchange in RoPE is negligible compared to the global-memory traffic it replaces, making fusion of RMSNorm+RoPE always beneficial for memory-bound workloads

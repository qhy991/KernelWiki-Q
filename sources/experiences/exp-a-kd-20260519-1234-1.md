## Solution

Replace inverted-scale multiplication with direct per-element division and introduce a min_scaling_factor floor (1/(FP8_E4M3_MAX*512)) to cap the scaling factor, trading a slight latency increase from division for improved numerical stability and near-zero protection

## Key Lessons

- In memory-bound kernels with low compute-to-memory ratio (e.g., 2 elements per thread with one load and one store each), replacing multiplication with division has negligible performance impact because memory latency dominates over instruction latency — apply when numerical stability benefits justify the change
- A min_scaling_factor floor in per-token quantization prevents extreme scaling when absmax approaches zero, avoiding overflow and preserving meaningful dynamic range — applies to any per-token or per-channel quantization scheme

## Solution

## Key Lessons

- Register-saving fusion by eliminating temporary arrays only yields measurable speedup when the eliminated registers would have caused spilling — with small 8-element fragments the compiler keeps all temporaries in the register file and schedules loads efficiently
- The CUTLASS LambdaIterator pattern of staging into separate fragments before bulk multiply is safe for small tiles but should be fused for larger fragment sizes that approach register file limits
- Microbenchmarks with small working sets may show neutral results for register optimizations that become significant at production-scale fragment and tile sizes

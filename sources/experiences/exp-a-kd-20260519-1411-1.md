## Challenge

The _f16Vec operator+= for float2 packs two scalar floats into a float2 temporary, adds component-wise via .x/.y field access, then deconstructs back to the scalar array, creating unnecessary abstraction overhead that the developer expected to be costly


## Solution

Specialize the operator+= with if constexpr (std::is_same_v<T2, float2>) to bypass float2 construction entirely and perform direct scalar addition (data[i] += other.data[i]), which is semantically clearer and avoids relying on the compiler to see through the float2 abstraction

## Key Lessons

- At -O3, nvcc sees through float2 construction/deconstruction overhead and compiles both the packed-temporary and direct-scalar patterns to identical SASS (two independent FADD instructions) — the compiler effectively eliminates the abstraction cost of packing scalars into structs for component-wise operations
- Use if constexpr specialization to express the direct-scalar path explicitly rather than relying on the optimizer to see through struct abstractions — while performance is equivalent, the intent is clearer and future compiler changes are less risky
- Measurement noise of ~0.1% is expected in microbenchmarks of simple arithmetic kernels where both paths compile to the same instruction sequence; do not over-interpret sub-1% deltas as meaningful performance differences

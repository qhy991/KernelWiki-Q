## Challenge

The Blelloch parallel prefix scan requires O(2·log N) __syncthreads() barriers across up-sweep and down-sweep phases, with indirect shared-memory addressing via offset*(2*tid+1)-1 indices that cause bank conflicts and irregular access patterns


## Solution

Replace the Blelloch algorithm with a two-level warp exclusive scan using __shfl_up_sync: per-warp scans via hardware shuffle instructions eliminate shared-memory bank conflicts, and a single cross-warp prefix step reduces total __syncthreads() barriers from O(2·log N) to approximately 5-6

## Key Lessons

- Use warp shuffle (__shfl_up_sync) for prefix scans within a warp instead of shared-memory Blelloch — shuffle instructions are register-level operations that avoid bank conflicts and __syncthreads() barriers entirely within the warp
- For scan sizes spanning multiple warps, use a two-level approach (per-warp scan + cross-warp prefix) to keep the algorithm work-efficient while minimizing __syncthreads() barriers from O(2·log N) to approximately O(log(num_warps))
- Eliminating indirect shared-memory addressing patterns like offset*(2*tid+1)-1 in favor of contiguous warp-level operations improves memory access regularity and reduces bank conflict risk

## Solution

Introduce a TopKPair struct tracking both max and secondMax per thread, with a custom TopKPairArgMax reducer that finds two top elements per BlockReduce call, halving iterations from k to ceil(k/2)

## Key Lessons

- Paired TopK reduction (tracking max + secondMax in a single struct and reducing together) halves BlockReduce iteration count but doubles per-call shared-memory footprint because TopKPair is twice the size of cub_kvp — net throughput is unchanged when total shared-memory traffic dominates
- When num_experts << BLOCK_SIZE (here 64 vs 256), 75% of threads are idle in the scanning loop, making per-call reduction overhead the dominant cost rather than iteration count — iteration-reducing optimizations produce no gain when the bottleneck is the reduction call itself

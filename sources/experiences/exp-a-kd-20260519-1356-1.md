## Challenge

Batched expert GEMM with asymmetric dimensions (M=32, N=3072) under tile <64,64,128> pads M=32 to 64, wasting 50% of computed rows and doubling the effective work for the small-M dimension


## Solution

Swap matrix operands (swap_ab) to reinterpret the GEMM as (N,K)x(K,M) with tile <128,16,256>, aligning the large N=3072 dimension with 128-wide tiles for zero padding waste and using 256-wide K tiles to halve K iteration steps

## Key Lessons

- For batched expert GEMM with highly asymmetric dimensions (small M, large N), swap the A/B matrix operands so the large dimension becomes the tile-M direction — this aligns the dominant dimension with wide tiles and eliminates padding waste
- Padding waste is multiplicative across all K steps: M=32 padded to 64 doubles every multiply-accumulate in the inner loop; reinterpreting as (N,K)x(K,M) with N=3072 aligned to 128-wide tiles achieves zero padding overhead
- Wider K tiles (256 vs 128) reduce the number of shared-memory load-compute-sync iterations, further improving throughput when the swap makes the padded shapes align to the larger K tile granularity

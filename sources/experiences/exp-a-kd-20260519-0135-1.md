## Solution

Divide mi by kLog2e before adding to logf(s_prime), undoing the log2 scaling so the final logsumexp is expressed in natural log space — a single-FLOP correctness fix with negligible performance cost (0.42% noise-level delta) since the 128 exp2f calls per thread dominate latency

## Key Lessons

- When mixing log2-scaled intermediates (mi = max * kLog2e) with natural-log functions (logf), the log2 scaling must be undone before combining — missing this conversion produces values inflated by kLog2e ≈ 1.44 and silently corrupts downstream numerics
- A single-precision division in the epilogue of a kernel dominated by exp2f/transcendental calls has negligible performance impact — correctness fixes in such regions are essentially free

## Challenge

When M dimension is smaller than 16 or not a multiple of 4, SM100 tensor cores cannot efficiently process the matrix. The previous approach padded the input in Python: allocating a larger tensor, zero-filling extra rows, computing GEMM on padded data, then slicing output back — adding memory allocation overhead, wasted computation on dummy rows, and an extra copy step


## Solution

Swap A/B matrices in GEMM dispatch when M < 16 or M % 4 != 0, computing B^T * A^T instead of A * B. This makes the weight dimension N the effective M in the kernel's view; since N is typically large and already a multiple of 4, it eliminates all Python-side padding overhead entirely

## Key Lessons

- Swap A/B matrix pointers and strides at the kernel dispatch level when the M dimension is small or misaligned — by computing B^T * A^T the large aligned N dimension becomes the effective M, eliminating Python-side padding overhead entirely
- The swap_ab technique requires coordinated changes across three layers: (1) dispatch condition and template selection, (2) MainloopArguments pointer/stride swap, and (3) problem shape and C-stride transposition — all three must be consistent for correctness
- On SM100 (Blackwell) the swap is handled natively by TCGEN05 without transposition overhead, but on SM80 the equivalent cublas OP_T path may regress; prefer this technique for SM100-targeted kernels or validate on target architecture

## Challenge

The single sm90_fp8_config_M64 config used TileShape (64x64x128) and ClusterShape (1x8x1) with Pingpong schedule for all M≤64 workloads regardless of N dimension, wasting computation on small-N problems and suffering from low arithmetic intensity due to shallow K-tile depth and Pingpong overhead


## Solution

Split into four N-aware configs: narrower N-tile (16 vs 64) for small N reduces wasted computation, deeper K-tile (256 vs 128) increases arithmetic intensity, switch from Pingpong to plain WarpSpecialized schedule eliminates overlap overhead for tiny kernels, and swap_ab=true (last template arg) improves TMA access patterns when M is small

## Key Lessons

- Narrow the N-tile dimension (e.g., 64→16) for small-N GEMM workloads to reduce wasted computation and improve tile occupancy — applies whenever N is much smaller than the default tile N dimension
- Deepen the K-tile (e.g., 128→256) to increase arithmetic intensity and amortize memory access overhead — beneficial when the kernel is memory-bound with shallow K coverage
- Prefer KernelTmaWarpSpecialized over KernelTmaWarpSpecializedPingpong for small-M kernels: Pingpong adds warp-specialization overlap overhead that cannot be amortized when there is insufficient work to overlap

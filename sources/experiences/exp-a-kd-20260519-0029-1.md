## Challenge

The split-KV heuristic computes split_wave_aware without an upper bound, which can over-allocate splits for large K scenarios and hurt performance due to excessive atomicAdd contention and redundant partial-output storage


## Solution

Cap split_wave_aware at sm_count/2 to prevent over-splitting when K is large; in this small-K=1024 case split_kv=8 is already well below sm_count/2=54, so the cap never activates and performance is identical (neutral verdict)

## Key Lessons

- Capping split_kv at sm_count/2 is a safety heuristic that prevents excessive atomic contention for large K but has no effect when K is small and split_wave_aware already falls below the cap — confirmed neutral (delta<0.2%) in this baseline scenario
- When validating heuristic changes, include a no-effect baseline case (small K where the heuristic does not trigger) to confirm the change is correctly a no-op and does not introduce unintended side effects

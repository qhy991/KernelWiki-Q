## Solution

Replace the 2-byte half scale load with a 1-byte ScaleFP8 load followed by a constant-memory LUT lookup (fp8_scale_lut[scales_fp8[tid].bits]) to halve scale tensor bandwidth, trading one global load of 2 bytes for one global load of 1 byte plus one constant-memory access

## Key Lessons

- Reducing scale precision from fp16 to fp8 halves scale tensor bandwidth but adds a constant-memory LUT access per scale — the net benefit depends on the compute-to-scale ratio; when the scale tensor is small relative to weight and compute traffic, the LUT overhead can offset the bandwidth savings entirely
- In real-world kernels like Marlin with fp4 weights (2 values per element vs 1 for int4), the higher compute-to-scale ratio increases the relative impact of scale bandwidth reduction, potentially making fp8 scales worthwhile even with LUT overhead
- Constant-memory LUT lookups are fast (cached, broadcast to warps) but still cost one memory instruction per unique index; evaluate the total instruction count change, not just the byte-count reduction, when trading precision for indirection

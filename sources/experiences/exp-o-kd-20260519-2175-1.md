## Solution

Eliminate compute_expert_offsets and compute_arg_sorts kernels entirely, reducing the 3-kernel pipeline to a single compute_problem_sizes launch — removing two kernel launch overheads and the expensive atomicAdd-based scatter-sort across 64 expert blocks that accounted for the majority of execution time

## Key Lessons

- Eliminate downstream pipeline stages when their outputs are not consumed by the caller — compute_expert_offsets and compute_arg_sorts produced expert_offsets, input_permutation, and output_permutation that were never needed downstream, so the entire 2-kernel suffix was dead work
- Kernel launch overhead (driver submission, grid setup) is significant for short-duration kernels — a single-thread serial prefix sum kernel (compute_expert_offsets<<<1,1>>>) costs more in launch overhead than in computation, making it a prime elimination target
- AtomicAdd-based scatter operations (compute_arg_sorts) create contention across warps competing for the same expert counters — removing the entire scatter step avoids both the atomic contention and the extra kernel launch

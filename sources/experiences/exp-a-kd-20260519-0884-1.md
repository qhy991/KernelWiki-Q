## Solution

Detect runs of consecutive pages (where both src_idx and dst_idx advance by 1) and merge each run into a single cudaMemcpy of size run_length × PAGE_BYTES, reducing 48 individual 8KB calls down to 6 merged 64KB transfers and eliminating 42 redundant dispatch roundtrips

## Key Lessons

- Merge contiguous page/device-memory copy operations into a single cudaMemcpy call sized to run_length × element_size — each synchronous cudaMemcpy incurs fixed driver dispatch overhead regardless of transfer size, so batching contiguous transfers amortizes that cost over more bytes
- Per-call dispatch overhead dominates total latency when individual transfer sizes are small (8KB here) — the 6.6x speedup is roughly proportional to the reduction from 48 calls to 6, confirming the bottleneck is dispatch count, not bandwidth
- Run-length detection (checking src_idx[i+1]-src_idx[i]==1 && dst_idx[i+1]-dst_idx[i]==1) is the minimal condition for safe merging — it ensures both source and destination ranges are contiguous so the merged memcpy produces identical results

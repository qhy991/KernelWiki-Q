## Solution

Add kClearLastStage pattern: explicitly zero the next-stage SMEM slot (the stage after the last written tile) after the mainloop body, ensuring no stale data remains when the circular buffer wraps around in future iterations — this is a correctness mechanism with negligible performance overhead since zeroing 128 floats (512 bytes) is dwarfed by the total mainloop SMEM traffic

## Key Lessons

- kClearLastStage is a correctness mechanism for circular SMEM buffers, not a performance optimization — it zeroes the next-stage slot to prevent stale data from leaking into future iterations, with negligible overhead (<0.1%) since clearing one stage's worth of data is small compared to total mainloop SMEM traffic
- When evaluating SMEM zeroing overhead, compare against total SMEM traffic across all stages — zeroing one stage of 128 floats (512 bytes) is noise when the mainloop processes 20 tiles across 4 stages
- An extra __syncthreads for a small zeroing step does not increase the critical-path stall when it matches the barrier cadence already established by the mainloop body

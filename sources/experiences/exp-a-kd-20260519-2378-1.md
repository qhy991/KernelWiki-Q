## Solution

Replace the tanh-approximation GELU with the exact GELU using ::erf, which requires only one FMUL (f * ALPHA) before the single SFU call and 3 final arithmetic ops — eliminating the x^3 computation, its associated __half-to-float casts, and reducing total arithmetic from 5+1 SFU to 4+1 SFU per element

## Key Lessons

- Prefer the exact GELU via ::erf over the tanh approximation (gelu_new) — ::erf requires fewer arithmetic instructions (4 vs 5) before the SFU call, reducing register pressure and latency in memory-bound activation kernels
- The tanh GELU approximation's x^3 computation introduces extra __half-to-float casts and intermediate multiplies in half-precision kernels that the erf path avoids entirely — the approximation was designed for training compatibility but costs more in hardware instructions
- When an activation function is the inner-loop body of a memory-bound kernel, even small reductions in arithmetic count (1-2 instructions per element) can yield measurable speedups by improving compute-to-memory overlap

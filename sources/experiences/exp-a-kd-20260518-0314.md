## Solution

Keep the running sum in a dedicated fp32 accumulator buffer throughout all rounds, then cast to fp16 in a single separate kernel after accumulation completes, eliminating repeated half conversions from the hot loop entirely

## Key Lessons

- Accumulate in fp32 and cast to reduced precision only once at the end rather than converting on every iteration — avoids 2N type-conversion round-trips through the narrow type — applies whenever a loop accumulates into a lower-precision output buffer
- Splitting a monolithic kernel into specialized phases (init/accumulate/cast) lets each phase operate on its native precision without interference — applies when precision boundaries do not align with computation boundaries

## Challenge

MoE expert routing pipeline launches 3 separate CUDA kernels (compute_problem_sizes, compute_expert_offsets, compute_arg_sorts) for only 8 experts and 64 tokens, where kernel launch overhead and stream synchronization cost dominate actual computation


## Solution

Eliminate 2 out of 3 kernel launches by skipping compute_expert_offsets and compute_arg_sorts entirely — at small scale (8 experts, 64 tokens) the per-kernel launch overhead (~2–5 μs each) exceeds the compute cost of the prefix-sum and atomic sort, so removing redundant launches yields a direct latency reduction

## Key Lessons

- For small-scale GPU workloads (few experts, few tokens), kernel launch overhead dominates compute — evaluate whether downstream kernels (prefix-sum, atomic sort) are truly needed or can be deferred to a later pipeline stage, as eliminating unnecessary launches can halve total latency
- When a multi-kernel pipeline's intermediate results (expert_offsets, permutations) are only consumed by a subsequent stage that can recompute them on-demand, prefer the single-kernel path and move the recomputation to the consumer — trading redundant compute for fewer kernel launches is profitable at small problem sizes

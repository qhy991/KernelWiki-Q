## Challenge

Warp-level sum reduction uses __shfl_down_sync followed by a separate __shfl_sync broadcast to distribute the result from lane 0 to all threads, requiring 6 total shuffle operations (5 shfl_down + 1 broadcast) per reduction call


## Solution

Replace __shfl_down_sync sequential reduction plus explicit __shfl_sync broadcast with __shfl_xor_sync butterfly pattern, where after 5 XOR-shuffle steps all 32 threads hold the correct sum simultaneously, eliminating the 6th broadcast instruction

## Key Lessons

- __shfl_xor_sync butterfly pattern leaves all threads holding the correct result after reduction, eliminating the separate __shfl_sync broadcast that __shfl_down_sync requires — saves one shuffle instruction per reduction call
- A single shuffle instruction savings (5 vs 6 ops) is typically too small to produce measurable speedup when dwarfed by memory access, loop control, and other kernel overhead — instruction-count reduction alone does not guarantee performance gain

## Challenge

FP8-to-FP16 scale dequant splits a 32-bit register into lower and upper 16-bit halves with separate mask-and-shift operations (12+ source-level integer instructions per dequant call), creating unnecessary instruction verbosity that obscures the underlying bit-manipulation intent


## Solution

Exploit the 1-bit exponent difference between FP8 E4M3 and FP16: a single combined mask (q & 0xFF00FF00) >> 1 simultaneously extracts both FP8 exponent/mantissa fields from the upper byte of each 16-bit lane and shifts them into FP16 position, reducing 12 source-level integer operations to 4

## Key Lessons

- Use combined interleaved masks (e.g., 0xFF00FF00) to operate on both 16-bit lanes of a 32-bit register simultaneously — avoids redundant split/mask/shift/repack sequences when the same bit operation applies to both lanes
- NVCC -O3 will often converge per-half and combined bit-manipulation patterns to equivalent SASS for simple integer ALU operations, so the primary benefit of the combined pattern is source-level clarity and maintainability rather than runtime speedup
- FP8 E4M3 to FP16 conversion requires only a 1-bit right-shift of the upper byte because FP16 has one more exponent bit than FP8 E4M3 — understanding the IEEE 754 bit-layout relationship enables the single-shift dequant trick

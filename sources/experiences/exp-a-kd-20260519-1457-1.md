## Solution

Fuse renormalization into the topk selection loop: thread 0 accumulates selected_sum during the 4 topk iterations (negligible extra work since k is small), then divides the 4 output weights by selected_sum in a single-thread epilogue — eliminating the second kernel launch and the 128KB intermediate weights global memory round-trip

## Key Lessons

- Fuse lightweight post-processing (renormalization of k=4 elements) into the primary kernel loop instead of launching a separate kernel — eliminates kernel launch overhead and avoids writing then re-reading intermediate results from global memory, yielding significant speedup when the intermediate data volume (128KB) is non-trivial relative to compute
- When the fused post-processing work is O(k) per row with small k (k=4), a single-thread epilogue (threadIdx.x == 0 only) is sufficient and avoids the atomicAdd and extra synchronization overhead that the original multi-thread renormKernel required

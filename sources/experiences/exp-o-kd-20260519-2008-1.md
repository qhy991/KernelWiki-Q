## Challenge

Batched expert GEMM with M=4, N=3072, K=1536 uses direct (M,K)x(K,N) tiling with <64,64,128> padding, which pads M=4 to 64 rows — wasting 60 out of 64 computed rows (93.75% waste in the M dimension) and multiplying total padded work by 16x versus the useful computation


## Solution

Apply swap_ab to reinterpret the GEMM as (N,K)x(K,M), making the large dimension N=3072 the effective M (no padding waste) and the small M=4 the effective N (pads to only 16), then swap A and B matrices in host packing and un-transpose the result C back to (M,N) order, reducing total padded work from 64*3072*1536 to 3072*16*1536 (4x reduction)

## Key Lessons

- For batched GEMM with very small M and large N, apply swap_ab to transpose the problem so the large dimension becomes M and the small dimension becomes N — this minimizes padding waste because tile sizes pad small N efficiently but small M catastrophically
- When swapping GEMM dimensions, the host packing must swap A and B (copy B into the padded A buffer and A into the padded B buffer with transposed indexing) and the result must be un-transposed from (N,M) back to (M,N) layout
- Tile padding overhead is proportional to (pad_M*pad_N*pad_K) / (M*N*K); for M=4 with 64-wide tiles the overhead is 16x, while padding N=4 to 16 is only 4x — always put the small dimension in the position with the smaller tile granularity

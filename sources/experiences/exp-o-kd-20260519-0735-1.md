## Solution

Replace shared-memory Blelloch up-sweep/down-sweep with barrier-free __shfl_up_sync-based warp exclusive scan, using a two-level aggregation (per-warp prefix via warp_exclusive_scan + warp0 cross-warp exclusive scan on warp_sums) to reduce total __syncthreads() from 16 to 7

## Key Lessons

- Replace shared-memory Blelloch prefix scan with __shfl_up_sync-based warp exclusive scan to eliminate all intra-warp __syncthreads() barriers — warp shuffle instructions execute in lockstep within a warp without any synchronization overhead
- Use a two-level scan decomposition (per-warp exclusive scan via SHFL_UP, then warp0 aggregates warp-level partial sums) to handle scan sizes spanning multiple warps while keeping total __syncthreads() at O(num_warps) instead of O(2*log(scan_size))
- At small-to-medium scan sizes (64-256 elements) the barrier reduction dominates performance gains because each eliminated __syncthreads() saves the cost of synchronizing hundreds of threads across warps

## Solution

Replace the 128-iteration sub-byte element loop with a 4-iteration word-level loop that applies bitwise OR directly on uint32_t storage words via raw_data(), eliminating all shift-mask decode and read-modify-write encode overhead

## Key Lessons

- For bitwise operations (AND, OR, XOR) on packed sub-byte arrays, operate directly on storage words instead of unpacking individual elements — bitwise operators are naturally word-aligned and require no masking
- Sub-byte element accessors (get/set with shift-mask) should be avoided in hot loops whenever the operation semantics allow word-level equivalents — the 32x reduction in loop iterations (128 elements → 4 words) yields proportional instruction savings

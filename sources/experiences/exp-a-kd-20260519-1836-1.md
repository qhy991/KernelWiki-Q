## Solution

Algebraically fold the bias subtraction into the zero-point term: skip bias subtraction inside dequant (skip_flop=true), then compute zp_adjusted = zp + 8.0*scale so that nibble*scale - (zp + 8*scale) == (nibble-8)*scale - zp, eliminating one FP subtraction stage at the dequant level

## Key Lessons

- Algebraic folding of a constant bias term into the zero-point (zp_adjusted = zp + bias*scale) eliminates a subtraction inside dequant — applies whenever dequant applies a fixed bias that is later multiplied by scale, allowing the bias to be absorbed into the zero-point computation
- This optimization yields measurable savings in lop3/bitwise dequant paths (e.g., Marlin kernels) where skip_flop=true removes an __hsub2 from a tightly pipelined inner loop, but shows neutral results in simplified float-conversion harnesses that do not replicate the bitwise instruction-level savings

## Challenge

A heuristic splits the K dimension into 4 slices when k > 3*n, issuing four cuBLAS Sgemm calls into separate partial-result buffers and then reducing them with a dedicated kernel — adding 3 extra kernel launches, a 4x workspace allocation, and a reduction pass that together outweigh any benefit of smaller per-split working sets


## Solution

Remove the split-K heuristic entirely and issue a single cuBLAS Sgemm with the full K dimension on a dedicated CUDA stream, eliminating extra kernel launches, the partial-result buffer, and the reduction kernel — cuBLAS internally handles K=4096 efficiently enough that manual decomposition only adds overhead

## Key Lessons

- Removing a manual split-K decomposition in favor of a single cuBLAS call improves performance when K is moderate (≤4096), because cuBLAS's internal tiling already handles large K efficiently and the overhead of extra launches, partial buffers, and reduction outweighs any per-split working-set benefit
- Prefer a dedicated CUDA stream with cublasSetStream for sequential GEMM+custom-kernel pipelines — it avoids the default-stream synchronization overhead and keeps the GEMM and post-processing kernels on the same queue
- Avoid heuristic branching inside the hot loop (split-K vs single-GEMM) when the library's internal scheduler already selects an optimal algorithm — the branch itself prevents kernel launch overlap and adds control-flow latency

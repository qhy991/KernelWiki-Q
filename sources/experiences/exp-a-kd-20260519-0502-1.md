## Solution

Switch R_D accumulator storage from FP16 (2 uint32_t per element) to FP32 (4 uint32_t per element) by setting kOStorageAccFloat32=1, eliminating __half2float/__float2half_rn conversion pairs in the P@V rescaling loop and final normalization, at the cost of doubling R_D register usage from 16 to 32 registers for kWarpTileHeadDimV=8

## Key Lessons

- Storing the output accumulator in FP32 instead of FP16 eliminates __half2float/__float2half_rn conversion overhead in rescaling loops, but the benefit is proportionally small when HMMA tensor-core MMA operations dominate total execution time — applies only when rescaling frequency is high relative to compute density
- Doubling R_D register footprint from 16 to 32 registers (for kWarpTileHeadDimV=8 with head_dim=64) increases register pressure that can offset conversion savings; the net effect depends on whether the kernel is register-bound or compute-bound
- For FlashAttention kernels with small sequence lengths and moderate head dimensions, FP32 accumulator storage yields neutral performance because the instruction savings from eliminated conversions are counterbalanced by increased register allocation and potential occupancy reduction

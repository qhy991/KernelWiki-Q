## Challenge

FlashAttention-2 kernel uses kStage=1 sequential K/V loading where each gmem-to-smem transfer fully blocks with CP_ASYNC_WAIT_GROUP(0) before computation can proceed, preventing any overlap between async memory transfers and MMA math operations


## Solution

Upgrade to kStage=2 multi-stage async prefetch by issuing V gmem-to-smem before Q@K^T MMA to overlap transfer with compute, and prefetching next K tile during softmax rescale; however this required halving Bc from 64 to 32 to fit double-buffered smem tiles, which doubled K/V loop iterations and increased overhead beyond the latency-hiding benefit at head_dim=64, seqlen=512

## Key Lessons

- Multi-stage async prefetch (kStage=2) only helps when per-tile MMA work is large enough to hide the gmem transfer latency — for small tiles (head_dim=64, seqlen=512) the computation is too short to overlap the full transfer, negating the benefit
- Reducing tile size (Bc) to fit double-buffered smem can be a net negative: halving Bc doubles the number of loop iterations, increasing barrier synchronization, online-softmax round-trips, and loop overhead that may exceed the latency-hiding gain
- Prefetch eligibility constraints (smem must fit 2x K/V tiles) can force suboptimal tiling — verify that the resulting tile dimensions do not degrade arithmetic intensity or increase iteration count beyond what the overlap saves

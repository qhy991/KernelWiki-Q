## Challenge

Blelloch exclusive prefix scan for MoE expert routing uses shared-memory up-sweep and down-sweep phases requiring 10 __syncthreads barriers across 1024 threads, incurring heavy synchronization cost for scan_size=16 which fits within a single warp


## Solution

Replace Blelloch shared-memory scan (8 scan-phase + 2 auxiliary __syncthreads = 10 barriers) with a two-level warp shuffle exclusive scan using __shfl_up_sync, which executes barrier-free within the warp and reduces total barriers to 7, yielding ~10% latency reduction for scan_size=16 with 1024 threads

## Key Lessons

- When scan_size fits within a single warp (N <= 32), warp shuffle (__shfl_up_sync) based exclusive scan replaces Blelloch's up-sweep/down-sweep with barrier-free intra-warp reduction — applies to MoE expert prefix sums and any small-N prefix scan where warp-level communication suffices
- Each __syncthreads at 1024 threads costs hundreds of cycles for full-block barrier synchronization; replacing multiple shared-memory scan phases with warp shuffle calls eliminates 3 of 10 barriers for a measurable ~10% latency win, demonstrating that barrier count reduction is a first-class optimization target in small-data-parallel kernels

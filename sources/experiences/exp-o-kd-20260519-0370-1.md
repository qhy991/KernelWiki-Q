## Solution

Promote the output accumulator R_D from FP16 storage (2 regs) to FP32 storage (4 regs), eliminating all per-iteration __half2float/__float2half_rn conversion overhead in the online rescaling loop; conversion to half is deferred to a single final rescale step after all K-tiles are processed

## Key Lessons

- Use FP32 accumulator storage for output in tiled attention kernels that perform per-iteration online rescaling — the FP16→float→FMA→half round-trip wastes instructions each iteration while FP32 storage does a single __fmaf_rn and defers the half conversion to the final epilogue, saving O(Tc) conversion pairs per accumulator element
- Deferring precision downcast to the final step (after all K-tiles) also avoids intermediate FP16 rounding between rescale steps, improving numerical accuracy at negligible register cost when the warp has spare registers

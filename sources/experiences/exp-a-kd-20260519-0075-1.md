## Solution

Replace the 128-iteration element-by-element sub-byte extraction and insertion loop with a direct word-level AND over the 4 underlying uint32_t storage words via raw_data(), eliminating all shift-mask-read-modify-write overhead and reducing logical operations by 32x

## Key Lessons

- For packed sub-byte data types (1-bit, 4-bit, etc.), always prefer word-level bulk operations over per-element extraction/insertion when the operation is bitwise-compatible — a single AND on uint32_t replaces 32 individual shift-mask-OR sequences
- CUTLASS's packed storage design exposes raw_data() precisely to enable this optimization: any commutative bitwise operation (AND, OR, XOR) on packed representations can be applied at the storage-word level without unpacking, yielding orders-of-magnitude speedups

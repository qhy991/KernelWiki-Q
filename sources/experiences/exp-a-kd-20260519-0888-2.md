## Challenge

Shared-memory reduction with __syncthreads barriers in per-token-group quantization incurs synchronization overhead and shared-memory bank contention, disproportionately costly for small group_size=64 where reduction time dominates total kernel execution time


## Solution

Replace shared-memory reduction with warp-shuffle __shfl_xor_sync reduction (GroupReduceMax) across 16 threads per group, eliminating all __syncthreads barriers and shared-memory usage entirely while supporting multiple independent groups per warp/block

## Key Lessons

- Warp-shuffle reduction (__shfl_xor_sync/__shfl_down_sync) replaces shared-memory plus __syncthreads for reductions within a single warp — eliminates shared-memory bank contention and barrier overhead, especially beneficial when reduction cost is significant relative to total work
- Hardcoded warp-shuffle reduction masks (0xffff for 16 threads) allow multiple independent sub-warp groups to reduce in parallel within the same warp, enabling multi-group-per-block designs without cross-group interference

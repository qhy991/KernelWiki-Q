## Challenge

Two separate BlockReduce passes for min and max each require full block-level reduction with a __syncthreads() barrier between them, doubling synchronization overhead in the statistics phase of per-token int8 quantization


## Solution

Merge min and max tracking into a single MinMax struct and perform one BlockReduce<MinMax> pass with a custom reduction operator that combines both min and max simultaneously, eliminating one full reduction pass and one __syncthreads() barrier

## Key Lessons

- Merge independent reductions (min + max) into a single struct-based reduction to cut BlockReduce passes and __syncthreads() barriers in half — applies whenever two or more commutative reductions share the same input traversal
- Pack tightly coupled scalar reductions (min/max, sum/count) into a struct with a single BlockReduce to reduce shared-memory TempStorage footprint and synchronization overhead

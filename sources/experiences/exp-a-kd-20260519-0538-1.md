## Challenge

Warp swizzle optimization attempts to reverse MMA N-dimension iteration for odd warp rows via j_s=((i%2)&&WARP_SWIZZLE)?(WARP_TILE_N-j-1):j, aiming to diversify B-register consumption patterns and reduce shared-memory bank conflicts during ldmatrix, but the conditional and reversed indexing introduce overhead that outweighs any bank-conflict benefit


## Solution

Enable WARP_SWIZZLE template parameter to alternate MMA iteration direction for odd i rows, reversing j index order (j_s = WARP_TILE_N-j-1) to diversify B-matrix register consumption and reduce shared-memory bank conflicts during ldmatrix loads — however, on A800 (sm_80) with LDMATRIX_X4, the hardware already handles bank conflicts efficiently, and the conditional (i%2) branch prevents full pragma unroll while disrupting HMMA register-reuse scheduling, causing a net 10% regression

## Key Lessons

- Warp-level index swizzle (alternating MMA iteration direction via conditional j_s) can regress performance on sm_80+ because LDMATRIX_X4 already handles bank conflicts efficiently — the added conditional branch prevents pragma unroll and disrupts HMMA instruction scheduling, producing net negative overhead
- Conditional (i%2) branching inside tightly-unrolled MMA loops prevents the compiler from fully optimizing register reuse and instruction scheduling — avoid data-dependent branching in innermma loops unless the hardware genuinely cannot resolve the target bank-conflict pattern
- Not all bank-conflict mitigation techniques are beneficial across GPU architectures; ldmatrix on sm_80+ has its own internal access pattern that may already be optimal, making software-level swizzle redundant and harmful

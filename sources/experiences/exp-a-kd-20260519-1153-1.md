## Challenge

The naive dot product for Float8_ (4 float2 pairs) computes 4 independent mul operations producing 4 float2 intermediate registers (8 FP32 registers total), then tree-adds them with separate add calls, resulting in high register pressure and 6 mul + 6 add instructions instead of 6 fused multiply-add instructions


## Solution

Replace the mul-then-tree-add pattern with a serial FMA chain: multiply the first pair into an accumulator, then fuse the remaining 3 multiply-add pairs via FMA intrinsics, reducing register pressure from 8 to 2 FP32 registers and replacing 6 mul+6 add instructions with 6 FMA instructions

## Key Lessons

- Replacing separate mul+add trees with FMA chains reduces register pressure (8 → 2 FP32 registers for Float8_ dot) and instruction count (12 → 6 ops) by fusing multiply and add into single hardware instructions — applies whenever computing sums of products with independent term groups
- In microbenchmarks dominated by global memory loads, FMA register savings show minimal speedup (~1.3%); the benefit becomes significant inside tight GEMM inner loops with high compute intensity where register pressure limits occupancy and instruction-level parallelism

## Solution

Right-size the GEMM configuration for small M by switching to 128x128 tiles with 1x1 cluster (single SM per CTA), eliminating wasted thread lanes in the oversized M tile and removing cluster synchronization overhead

## Key Lessons

- Right-size tile and cluster dimensions to the actual problem geometry — for small M (e.g., M≤16), large M tiles (256) with multi-SM clusters waste most thread lanes on bounds-checking and add synchronization overhead; prefer small tiles (128) with single-SM clusters
- Multi-SM cluster shapes (e.g., 4x4 = 16 SMs) incur coordination cost that is only amortized when the tile is fully utilized; when the M dimension is much smaller than the tile M, the cluster overhead dominates and degrades performance

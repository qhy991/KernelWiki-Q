## Solution

Promote num_tokens to a compile-time template parameter kNumTokens, enabling exact-sized accumulators (float acc[kNumTokens]), full #pragma unroll on all token loops across the compute, warp shuffle, and cross-warp reduction sections, plus __launch_bounds__(128,1) for occupancy tuning

## Key Lessons

- Promoting small runtime dimensions (e.g., batch size M) to compile-time template constants enables NVCC to fully unroll all dependent loops, allocate exact-sized register arrays, and eliminate branch overhead — yielding large speedups when the dimension is small (≤16) and known at kernel launch time
- Over-allocated accumulator arrays (float acc[16] for actual M=8) waste registers and prevent optimal register allocation; compile-time sizing to kNumTokens reduces register pressure and improves occupancy
- Adding __launch_bounds__(blockSize, minBlocks) after compile-time specialization gives the compiler a concrete occupancy target, enabling better register spilling and instruction scheduling decisions

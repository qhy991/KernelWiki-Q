## Solution

Replace the 128-iteration element-by-element loop with a 4-iteration loop over the underlying uint32_t storage words accessed via raw_data(), performing a single bitwise AND per word that processes 32 packed bits simultaneously and eliminates all sub-byte extraction and insertion overhead

## Key Lessons

- For bitwise operations on packed sub-byte arrays, bypass sub-byte element accessors (get/set) and operate directly on storage words via raw_data() — a single word-level AND processes 32 packed 1-bit elements at once, replacing 32 shift-mask-OR sequences with one instruction
- When data is already packed into power-of-two-width words, bitwise operations (AND/OR/XOR/NOT) are trivially parallelizable across all packed elements within each word — prefer whole-word operations over per-element extraction whenever the operation is bitwise and alignment allows

## Challenge

The fp8 blockwise GEMM dispatch uses a k > 3*n heuristic to select StreamKScheduler for tall-skinny matrices and PersistentScheduler otherwise, introducing dispatch complexity tied to a specific forked kernel variant that may not be optimal for all problem shapes


## Solution

Remove the conditional StreamK/Persistent scheduler heuristic and hardcode a single cooperative warp-specialized configuration using KernelTmaWarpSpecializedCooperativeFP8BlockScaledAccum with MmaTileShape 128x128x128 and ClusterShape 1x2x1, which handles varying problem shapes well enough that the StreamK split-K heuristic is unnecessary

## Key Lessons

- Removing shape-specific scheduler heuristics in favor of a single well-tuned cooperative warp-specialized kernel simplifies dispatch logic while maintaining or improving performance across problem shapes — applies when the upstream cooperative mainloop scheduler handles varying M/N/K ratios adequately
- Scheduler selection heuristics (e.g., k > 3*n → StreamK) are often tied to specific forked kernel variants and may not transfer to upstream cooperative implementations — always revalidate heuristics when switching underlying GEMM mainloop strategies

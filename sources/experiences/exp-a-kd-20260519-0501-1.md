## Challenge

FlashAttention-2 MMA kernel stores its output accumulator R_D in FP16 (half) format, requiring __half2float() and __float2half_rn() conversion round-trips during every online softmax rescaling step in the P@V accumulation loop, adding 8 conversion instructions per element per iteration


## Solution

Set kOStorageAccFloat32=1 to store the output accumulator R_D as 4 float registers (4×uint32_t) instead of 4 half registers (2×uint32_t), enabling direct __fmaf_rn() rescaling without any dtype conversion during the P@V accumulation and final output normalization

## Key Lessons

- Use FP32 registers for output accumulators in FlashAttention-style online-rescaling kernels to eliminate __half2float/__float2half_rn conversion overhead per rescaling step — the cost savings scales with the number of K-tile iterations where rescaling occurs
- FP16 accumulator storage saves registers (2 vs 4 uint32_t per accumulator slot) but the conversion round-trip cost in the rescaling loop dominates for small-to-moderate sequence lengths where the loop count is low relative to per-iteration instruction overhead

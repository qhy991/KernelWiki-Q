## Challenge

The flat warp-level TopK reduction iterates K times, each time scanning all N=8 per-thread candidates for the local best, performing a warp reduce, then invalidating the winner — resulting in K warp synchronizations and O(N*K) per-thread comparisons that motivated a grouped approach.


## Solution

Split N=8 candidates into 2 groups of 4, apply a sorting network (Sort4) and per-group reduceTopKFunc with sorted-shift invalidation, distribute partial results across warp lanes, then merge via a final reduceTopKFunc — but this triples the total warp synchronizations from K=4 to approximately 3*K=12, causing a 23.9% performance regression for small N and K.

## Key Lessons

- Hierarchical decomposition of warp-level reductions multiplies the number of warp synchronizations by the number of groups (here 2 group reductions + 1 merge = 3x the flat K synchronizations) — for small N and K, this synchronization overhead dominates any per-thread work savings from smaller candidate sets
- A flat K-iteration TopK with O(N) per-thread scan per iteration can outperform a grouped approach when K is small, because each flat iteration requires only one warp reduction regardless of N while grouping adds numLoops*K + K total reductions
- Sorting networks (Sort4) as a per-group preprocessing step do not amortize their instruction cost when the group count is small and the merge step reintroduces full warp reductions

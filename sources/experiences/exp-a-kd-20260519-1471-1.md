## Challenge

CUTLASS GEMM with M=96 uses default TileShape<128,128,64>, causing only 75% M-tile utilization (96/128 valid elements) and shallow K=64 pipeline staging, wasting tensor core capacity for small-M workloads


## Solution

Switch to TileShape<64,128,128> via N-dependent dispatch: tile M=64 better matches M=96 (two tiles with higher per-tile utilization), and doubling tile K from 64 to 128 deepens the MMA pipeline for improved throughput on small-M, moderate-N GEMM shapes

## Key Lessons

- Choose tile M dimension to divide evenly into (or closely match) problem M — a tile M=128 for M=96 wastes 25% of each tile, while tile M=64 maps to two well-utilized tiles
- Deepening tile K (64→128) increases pipeline depth and amortizes shared-memory load overhead, especially beneficial when M is small and N is moderate (<8192)
- N-dependent tile dispatch selects smaller-M/deeper-K configs for moderate-N regimes where the default large-M tile causes underutilization

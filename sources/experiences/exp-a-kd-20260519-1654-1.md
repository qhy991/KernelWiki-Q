## Challenge

CUTLASS GEMM with M=64 uses a fixed TileShape of 128x128x64, meaning only half the 128-row M tile contains valid data, wasting 50% of compute per threadblock and underutilizing tensor core capacity


## Solution

Adapt TileShape from 128x128x64 to 64x128x128, matching the M=64 dimension exactly to eliminate wasted compute, while doubling the K tile from 64 to 128 to improve pipeline depth and reduce startup overhead

## Key Lessons

- Adapt CUTLASS TileShape M dimension to match the actual batch dimension when M is small (<=128) — a tile larger than M wastes compute on invalid rows while a tile equal to M fully utilizes every threadblock
- Doubling the K-dimension tile (64→128) alongside an M-dimension reduction preserves overall tile volume and improves pipeline depth, partially compensating for reduced parallelism in the M direction
- Use M-adaptive tile configurations (e.g., sm80_config_M64) rather than a single fixed tile for all M sizes — the occupancy gain is proportional to how much of the original tile was wasted

## Solution

Introduce double-buffered smem partitions (kStage=2) with cp.async software pipelining: V is prefetched asynchronously before Q@K^T (no blocking wait), next-K is prefetched during P@V, and CP_ASYNC_WAIT_GROUP(1) before P@V ensures V is ready while keeping the next-K async group in flight

## Key Lessons

- Software pipelining with cp.async double-buffered smem partitions (kStage=2) can hide gmem latency by prefetching V during Q@K^T and next-K during P@V — applies to any tile-loop kernel with sequential gmem-to-smem loads where compute phases are long enough to overlap
- Async pipeline effectiveness depends on compute-to-memory ratio per tile: reducing Bc from 64 to 32 halves the MMA work per iteration, shortening the compute phase that hides gmem transfer latency — pipeline depth and tile dimensions must be co-tuned rather than changed independently
- CP_ASYNC_WAIT_GROUP(n) with n=1 waits for all but the most recent async group, enabling fine-grained pipeline control where V is guaranteed ready before P@V while the next-K prefetch remains in flight — critical for correct multi-stage async scheduling

fp32_rms_norm_llama3_8b_hs4096

## Solution

Tried two variants: (1) butterfly xor reduction with __shfl_xor_sync, (2) down-shift reduction + smem weight cache + #pragma unroll 4 + __frsqrt_rn

## Key Lessons

- Butterfly xor reduction (__shfl_xor_sync) is slower than down-shift (__shfl_down_sync) for RMS norm on A800 — avoid for this workload
- Unroll 4 + __frsqrt_rn gives only ~0.8% gain over unroll 2 + __fdividef — diminishing returns
- Single-row-per-block approach is near-optimal at 2.323x; further gains require multi-row or persistent kernel strategies
- Shared memory weight cache (16KB smem_w) is the most impactful optimization found so far
- Next promising hypotheses: persistent kernel with grid-stride loop, 2-rows-per-block for large batches, L2 prefetch hints

## Solution

Decompose each packed half2 __hfma2 into two scalar FP32 fma operations: extract weight halves via __low2float/__high2float, convert activations via __half2float, accumulate in float, then scale and convert back to half — improving per-dot-product accuracy (max rel error drops from 0.682 to 0.343) at the cost of doubled instruction count and added type-conversion overhead

## Key Lessons

- Packed half2 __hfma2 processes two products per instruction and avoids type-conversion overhead; scalar FP32 fma doubles the arithmetic count and adds __low2float/__high2float/__half2float conversions — use FP32 accumulation only when numerical precision requirements justify the throughput cost
- Converting from half2 packed math to scalar FP32 math increases register pressure (float registers are 2x wider than half2 pairs), reducing occupancy in register-bound kernels
- FP32 intermediate accumulation improves per-dot-product accuracy (max relative error halved from 0.682 to 0.343) but the 44% latency regression shows that in GPTQ-style dequant kernels the throughput loss from abandoning __hfma2 usually outweighs the precision gain

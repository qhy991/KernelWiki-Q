## Solution

Cap split_kv at sm_count/2 when split_wave_aware > 1, using min(split_wave_aware, sm_count/2) to prevent pathological over-splitting while preserving the wave-aware heuristic for normal cases

## Key Lessons

- Cap split_kv at sm_count/2 to prevent pathological over-splitting in attention kernels — the cap only activates when split_wave_aware exceeds the threshold, so normal cases are unaffected and only extreme K values benefit
- Uncapped heuristics that scale with K can silently degrade performance by producing more splits than the hardware can efficiently schedule, especially when fused_reduction forces atomic accumulation into a shared buffer
- Defensive capping of launch-configuration heuristics is essential even when the heuristic itself is well-motivated — the guard costs nothing when inactive but prevents worst-case behavior

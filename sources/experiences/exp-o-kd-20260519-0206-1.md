## Solution

Switch to CausalMask<true> (IsQBegin=true) which computes max_blocks_q = ceil_div((blk_coord_q+1)*tile_q, tile_k) without the offset_q term, reducing trip count from 250 to 10 since Q positioned at the beginning only needs a triangular subset of K-blocks

## Key Lessons

- Select CausalMask<true> (IsQBegin=true) instead of CausalMask<false> when Q is positioned at the beginning of the sequence — removing the offset_q = N_K - N_Q term from the trip count formula eliminates unnecessary K-block iterations and produces a triangular visit pattern that scales with O(Q) rather than O(K)
- The offset_q = N_K - N_Q term in CausalMask<false> shifts the upper-bound calculation so that every Q-row sees nearly all K-blocks; when N_K >> N_Q this inflates total work by a factor of ~N_K/N_Q — always verify whether the Q-begin assumption holds before choosing the mask variant
- Causal mask policy selection is a zero-cost algorithmic optimization (no code added, only a branch removed) that can yield orders-of-magnitude speedup for asymmetric Q/K dimensions — the dominant factor in FMHA tile-level work is the number of K-blocks visited per Q-block

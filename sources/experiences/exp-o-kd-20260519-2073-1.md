## Challenge

CUTLASS int8 GEMM with M=96 uses a default TileShape<128,128,64> that wastes 25% of the M-tile (only 96 of 128 elements are valid), forcing extra warps to process padding and underutilizing the tensor cores for this non-standard problem dimension


## Solution

Switch to TileShape<64,128,128> so that M=96 maps onto two 64-wide tiles (96/64≈1.5, high utilization) while deepening the K-dimension from 64 to 128 for better pipeline occupancy and shared-memory reuse in the N=2048 regime

## Key Lessons

- Match TileShape.M to the problem M so that the tile evenly divides or closely fits the actual dimension — a tile M larger than the problem M wastes warps on padding elements, reducing effective throughput
- For moderate M with moderate N (M<128, N<8192), prefer a smaller M tile paired with a deeper K tile (e.g., 64×128×128 instead of 128×128×64) to increase pipeline depth and shared-memory reuse without wasting M-direction occupancy
- CUTLASS tile shape selection should be dispatch-aware: different (M,N,K) regimes benefit from different TileShape parameters, and a single default shape leaves performance on the table for non-power-of-two or small-M workloads

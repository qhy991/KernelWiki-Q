## Solution

Replace __shfl_sync with a direct threadIdx.x / 32 computation wrapped in canonical_warp_idx(), which has no convergence requirement — each thread computes the division independently and proceeds immediately regardless of what other lanes are doing, eliminating both the shuffle instruction overhead and per-iteration reconvergence stalls

## Key Lessons

- Never use __shfl_sync to compute warp_idx when the value is thread-local (threadIdx.x / 32) — __shfl_sync forces a warp-level convergence barrier that costs ~20-40 cycles per invocation on Ampere, while a simple integer division has no cross-lane dependency and lets divergent threads proceed independently
- Prefer direct arithmetic over warp shuffle intrinsics when the result does not require data from another lane — shuffle operations impose reconvergence stalls that are especially costly inside loops with divergent control flow

## Solution

Replace the four __shfl_sync warp-shuffle instructions plus four scalar shared-memory stores with a single stmatrix.sync.aligned.x4.trans.m8n8.shared.b16 PTX instruction (SM90+), which performs hardware-accelerated 8x8 b16 matrix transpose during register-to-smem copy in one operation, with a scalar-store fallback for pre-SM90 hardware

## Key Lessons

- The SM90 stmatrix.sync.aligned.x4.trans.m8n8.shared.b16 instruction replaces 4 warp-shuffle + 4 scalar-store instructions with a single hardware transpose-and-store operation, eliminating shuffle latency and reducing instruction count — applies to any 8x8 b16 register-to-smem transpose pattern on Hopper GPUs
- Guard stmatrix usage with __CUDA_ARCH__ >= 900 and provide a scalar-store fallback to maintain correctness on pre-SM90 hardware, since the hardware transpose is only available on Hopper and later architectures
- Eliminating warp-shuffle instructions in transpose kernels also reduces register pressure (no intermediate t0-t3 temporaries) and potential shared-memory bank conflicts that scalar stores after shuffles may cause

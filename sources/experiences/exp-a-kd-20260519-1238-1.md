## Solution

Replace the hard-coded truncating-division mindiv with a loop-based ceiling-division scan over all 13 candidates using (N+nPrRnd-1)/nPrRnd, scanning backwards to find the largest i where rnds[0]==rnds[i] — mathematically cleaner but computes a smaller WvPrGrp that increases kernel iteration count for small M

## Key Lessons

- Ceiling division in grid-sizing helpers can compute a smaller blockDim than truncating division, reducing per-block wavefront count and increasing kernel loop iterations — always verify the resulting iteration count, not just mathematical correctness
- A single kernel iteration with idle wavefronts (244 of 756 threads exit early via the m<M guard) is faster than two full iterations when M is small relative to the grid stride, because the early-exit cost is negligible compared to reloading input and re-executing the reduction

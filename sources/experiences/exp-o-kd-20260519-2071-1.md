## Challenge

CUTLASS GEMM tile shape TileShape<128,128,64> with WarpShape<64,64,64> wastes 50% of warp M capacity when M=32, since each warp covers 64 M-elements but only 32 are valid, leaving half the warp idle


## Solution

Match TileShape M dimension exactly to the problem M=32 and increase K to 128 to maintain pipeline depth: TileShape<32,64,128> with WarpShape<32,64,64> achieves 100% warp M utilization and doubles the K staging to keep the tensor cores fed

## Key Lessons

- Match CUTLASS tile M dimension to the actual problem M to eliminate wasted warp capacity — when problem M is small (e.g., 32), a tile M of 128 leaves warps computing mostly padding, halving effective utilization
- When reducing tile M to match a small problem dimension, compensate by increasing tile K (e.g., 64→128) to maintain sufficient pipeline depth and keep tensor cores busy
- WarpShape M should divide into tile M exactly and should not exceed the problem M dimension; a warp covering 64 M-elements for M=32 wastes 50% of its arithmetic throughput

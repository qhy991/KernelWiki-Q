## Challenge

Tiled GEMM with M=2 padded to 64 in a 64x64 tile shape wastes 62 of 64 rows per output tile, computing 100M padded MACs per expert when only 3M real MACs are needed — a 33x overcompute ratio that saturates SMs with useless work


## Solution

Swap A/B operands to reformulate the GEMM as (N,K)x(K,M)=(1024,1536)x(1536,2), so the large dimension N=1024 fills tile rows efficiently while M=2 is padded to only 16, reducing padded MACs from 100M to 25M per expert — a 4x reduction in wasted computation

## Key Lessons

- Swap GEMM operands (AB → BA^T formulation) when one dimension is very small to move the small dimension into the tile-padding axis, drastically reducing padded waste — the tile-padding cost scales with the padded dimension, not the real one
- Padding M=2 to 64 wastes 97% of tile rows; padding M=2 to 16 wastes only 88% but critically reduces total padded volume by 4x because the large dimension now fills the other tile axis fully
- The AB-swap technique is mathematically free for GEMM (C=A·B implies C^T=B^T·A^T) but requires a post-GEMM transpose — amortize this cost when the GEMM itself is the bottleneck, especially in batched expert scenarios with many small-M problems

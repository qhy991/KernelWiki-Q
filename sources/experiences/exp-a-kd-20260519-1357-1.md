## Challenge

Grouped GEMM in MoE with small M=32 must pad the M dimension to PADDED_M=128 to satisfy cuBLAS tiling requirements, computing 4x more FLOPs than needed (33.6M vs 8.4M per expert) with 75% of computed rows discarded as zero-padded waste


## Solution

Apply the swap_ab transpose trick: compute C^T = B^T * A^T via cublasSgemm with CUBLAS_OP_T on both operands, making the large N=512 dimension the leading m parameter so cuBLAS sees m=512, n=32, k=256 — no M-padding required and all computed FLOPs are useful

## Key Lessons

- For small-M grouped GEMM in MoE scenarios, use the swap_ab trick (compute C^T = B^T * A^T) to make the large dimension the cuBLAS m parameter, eliminating wasteful M-padding — applies whenever M << N and the library requires a minimum m for efficient tiling
- The speedup from eliminating M-padding is less than the FLOP reduction ratio (1.22x vs 4x here) because the padded larger-m dimension allows cuBLAS better SM-level parallelism — the real gain is the net of wasted compute removed minus the parallelism lost
- cuBLAS grouped GEMM padding overhead scales with the waste ratio (PADDED_M / M); at moderate waste (4x) the improvement is modest (~1.22x), but at extreme waste (32x for very small M) the technique becomes critical

## Challenge

The MoE block-alignment kernel uses a Blelloch (work-efficient) parallel prefix sum in shared memory requiring O(log₂(scan_size)) __syncthreads() barriers in both the up-sweep and down-sweep phases, forcing all threads in the block to wait at each step even though most threads are idle during each iteration


## Solution

Replace the Blelloch shared-memory prefix sum with a two-level warp shuffle exclusive scan: intra-warp scans use warp_exclusive_scan (SHFL_UP, no barriers), per-warp partial sums are accumulated in warp_sums[], then warp0 performs a second-level exclusive scan on warp_sums[] — reducing __syncthreads() from ~2·log₂(scan_size) to ~5-6 fixed barriers regardless of scan_size

## Key Lessons

- Replace Blelloch shared-memory prefix sum with two-level warp shuffle exclusive scan when scan_size fits within a small number of warps — SHFL_UP instructions execute barrier-free within a warp, reducing __syncthreads() from O(log n) to O(1) and improving latency especially for n ≥ 16
- Decompose a block-level scan into per-warp sub-scans followed by a warp0 correction scan — this two-level pattern maps naturally to GPU warp hierarchy and avoids the serialization of the traditional Blelloch up-sweep/down-sweep phases
- Move aggregate computations (total sum, global counters) to immediately after the warp0 accumulation phase rather than after the full exclusive scan — downstream consumers of the total can proceed earlier without waiting for per-element prefix results

KernelBench level_1 task_9: Tall-and-skinny GEMM (16384x16 @ 16x16384) with K=16

## Key Lessons

- Vectorized loads (float4) can provide minor speedups even for small K dimensions
- For tall-and-skinny GEMM with small K, memory bandwidth is the main bottleneck
- K=16 being divisible by 4 enables clean vectorization without remainder handling

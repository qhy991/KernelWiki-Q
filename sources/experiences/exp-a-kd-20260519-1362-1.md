## Solution

Swap A/B operands via CUBLAS_OP_T on both matrices so the small M=4 dimension becomes N in the internal GEMM (compute B^T x A^T = (N,K)x(K,M) = (N,M)), allowing TILE_M=128 to cover the now-large M=4096 dimension and TILE_N=32 to efficiently handle the small dimension

## Key Lessons

- Swap A/B operands (CUBLAS_OP_T on both) to move a very small M dimension into the N slot so the large dimension maps to TILE_M, significantly improving tile utilization on SM120 CUTLASS where compile-time tile shapes are fixed — applies whenever M << TILE_M min and N is large
- On SM80 cuBLAS the swapAB transformation is neutral because cuBLAS internally handles both orientations with similar optimized paths; the benefit materializes primarily on CUTLASS-based SM120 pipelines where tile shape selection is compile-time constrained
- For small-M GEMM workloads, kernel launch overhead dominates total latency (output is only M*N = 16K elements), so algorithmic improvements may appear neutral in wall-clock time even when tile utilization improves substantially

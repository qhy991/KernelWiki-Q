## Solution

Replace the 128-iteration sub-byte element loop with a 4-iteration word-level loop that ORs entire uint32_t storage words directly, exploiting the fact that bitwise OR distributes over packed bits — eliminating all shift-mask and read-modify-write overhead

## Key Lessons

- Bitwise operations (AND, OR, XOR) on packed sub-byte data can be applied at the storage-word level instead of element-by-element — since these operations distribute over bit positions, word-level and element-level produce identical results with orders of magnitude fewer instructions
- Sub-byte extract (shift-mask) and insert (read-modify-write) loops are a major anti-pattern for bitwise boolean operations on packed arrays; always prefer whole-word operations when the operation is bitwise-parallel
- Loop iteration count reduction from N elements to N/32 storage words (128 → 4 in this case) directly reduces instruction count, improves register pressure, and can yield significant speedups on memory-bound or instruction-bound kernels

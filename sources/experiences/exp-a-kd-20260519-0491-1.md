## Solution

Switch R_D accumulator storage from FP16 (kOStorageAccFloat32=0) to FP32 (kOStorageAccFloat32=1) by expanding the register array from 2 to 4 uint32_t per element, enabling direct __fmaf_rn on float values during online rescaling and deferring the single float-to-half conversion to the final output store, eliminating 8 conversion instructions per K-tile iteration across 32 tiles

## Key Lessons

- Store MMA accumulator results in FP32 registers (4 uint32_t per 4-element group) instead of FP16 (2 uint32_t) when performing iterative online rescaling — eliminates __half2float/__float2half_rn conversion pairs each iteration, saving 8 conversion instructions per K-tile and avoiding intermediate FP16 rounding that accumulates error
- Defer the float-to-half conversion to the final output stage rather than converting at every rescaling step — this trades 2N register overhead for N×T conversion elimination where N is the output tile size and T is the number of K-tiles, which is a clear win for any T≥2
- The register overhead of FP32 accumulator storage (2x registers for R_D) is typically affordable in HMMA-based attention kernels because the accumulator register pressure is moderate relative to the 255-register budget, and the instruction savings directly reduce arithmetic intensity in the inner loop

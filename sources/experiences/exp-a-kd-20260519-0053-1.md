## Challenge

Conv3d fprop activation tile access uses plain integer division and modulo for NZPQ index decomposition on every access, compiling to six integer division instructions per element (~20 cycles each on SM80), making the kernel integer-ALU-bound


## Solution

Replace six plain div/mod operations with FastDivmod structs precomputed outside the loop that compile to __umulhi (single-cycle on SM80) plus a shift and multiply-subtract, reducing per-access index decomposition from ~120 cycles to ~9 cycles

## Key Lessons

- Replace hot-loop integer div/mod with FastDivmod (__umulhi-based reciprocal multiply) to reduce per-iteration cost from ~20 cycles per division to ~3 cycles — applies whenever loop-invariant divisors allow precomputation of the magic multiplier and shift
- Precompute divisor-dependent constants (magic multiplier, shift) outside tight loops rather than letting the compiler emit repeated integer division sequences — integer division is not pipelined on most GPU architectures and becomes the bottleneck in ALU-bound kernels

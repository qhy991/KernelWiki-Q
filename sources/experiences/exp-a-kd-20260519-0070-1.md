## Challenge

GEMM epilogue kernel issues two __syncthreads() barriers per single fragment — one before store-to-SMEM and one before load-from-SMEM — incurring 2*kIterations total barriers where the synchronization latency dominates the lightweight SMEM copy work between barriers


## Solution

Batch multiple fragments per barrier pair (FragmentsPerPartition=2): outer loop strides by FPP, inner loop stores two fragments into separate SMEM regions before a single __syncthreads, then loads two fragments after the next barrier, halving total barrier count from 2*kIterations to 2*(kIterations/FPP) while keeping per-fragment copy work unchanged

## Key Lessons

- Batch multiple SMEM fragments per __syncthreads() pair to reduce total barrier count — each eliminated barrier saves hundreds of cycles of warp stall, and the tradeoff is only increased SMEM usage proportional to the batching factor
- Increase FragmentsPerPartition to amortize barrier overhead when per-fragment SMEM copy work is lightweight relative to synchronization cost — applies to epilogue/store patterns in tiled GEMM where each fragment is small

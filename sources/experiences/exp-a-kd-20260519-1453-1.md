## Solution

Replace the FP16 intermediate buffer with FP32 storage, eliminating __float2half/__half2float conversion round-trips; the buffer doubles in size (8KB→16KB) but both fit in L2 cache, and direct float load/store removes conversion overhead while dramatically improving accumulation precision

## Key Lessons

- Replacing FP16 intermediate buffers with FP32 in multi-pass reduction eliminates float↔half conversion overhead and dramatically reduces accumulated rounding error (0.088%→0.0004%), though performance may stay neutral when both buffer sizes fit in L2 cache and the doubled bandwidth cost offsets the saved conversion instructions
- FP16 round-trip error compounds across accumulation passes — with 8 slices, up to 14 conversion operations per element amplify quantization noise, making FP32 intermediates essential for numerical accuracy even when latency is unchanged
- When intermediate buffers fit in L2 cache, the bandwidth difference between FP16 and FP32 storage is absorbed by cache hit rate, so precision gains come nearly for free; the tradeoff shifts when buffers exceed L2 capacity

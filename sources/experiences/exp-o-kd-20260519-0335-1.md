## Challenge

The accumulation kernel keeps its running sum in fp16, converting to fp32 and back to fp16 on every iteration of the hot loop, causing 2× redundant precision conversions and fp16 read-modify-write traffic per round


## Solution

Decompose the monolithic fp16-accumulating kernel into three separate kernels: init_zero (zero fp32 buffer), accumulate_float (pure fp32 hot loop with no conversions), and cast_output (single fp32→fp16 cast after all rounds), eliminating all per-iteration half conversions

## Key Lessons

- Keep accumulation loops entirely in fp32 and defer the fp16 cast to a separate pass — removing __half2float/__float2half from the hot loop eliminates 2× unnecessary conversion instructions and fp16 read-modify-write memory traffic per iteration
- Kernel decomposition (init → compute → cast) is preferable to fusing a narrow-format store into a wide-format compute loop when the compute-to-store ratio is high (64 rounds of accumulation vs 1 final store)

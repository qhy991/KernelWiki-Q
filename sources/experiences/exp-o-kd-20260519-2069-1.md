## Solution

Reduce tile M dimension from 128 to 16 via GemmShape<16,64,128> and WarpShape<16,64,64>, matching the tile M (16) close to the problem M (8) for 50% warp utilization instead of 12.5%, while deepening tile K from 64 to 128 to compensate for the smaller M and maintain pipeline throughput

## Key Lessons

- Match CUTLASS TileShape M dimension to the actual problem M to avoid wasted warp-level compute on padding — tile M should be the smallest power-of-2 ≥ problem M that satisfies CUTLASS alignment constraints
- When reducing tile M to match small problem dimensions, increase tile K proportionally (here 64→128) to deepen the MMA pipeline and compensate for reduced per-tile arithmetic intensity
- Small-M GEMM workloads are tile-shape-bound, not memory-bound — the dominant cost is predicated-out compute in oversized tiles, not data movement, so tile reshaping yields outsized speedups (2.4x here)

## Solution

Pre-invert the scale once per thread (1.0f / scale) and replace the per-element FP32 division with multiplication, reducing the expensive divide instruction from N invocations to a single pre-computation

## Key Lessons

- Replace per-element FP32 division with a single pre-inverted reciprocal followed by multiplication — FP32 divide has ~4x lower throughput than FMUL on most GPUs, so the amortization over N elements yields a net speedup even with one extra multiply for the reciprocal
- The speedup from instruction-level math optimizations (div→mul) is bounded by the fraction of total kernel time spent on that instruction path; at smaller problem sizes where memory and launch overhead dominate, the observable benefit shrinks proportionally

## Challenge

Shared-memory padding for B matrix bank-conflict avoidance in HGEMM TN adds 8 extra half-values per tile row, wasting ~50% of B smem capacity per tile while only providing marginal bank-conflict reduction on small problem sizes


## Solution

Replace B matrix shared-memory padding (B_PAD=8) with XOR-based swizzle address permutation via swizzle_permuted_B_j, which redistributes 8-byte chunks across banks without reserving extra columns, reclaiming the ~50% wasted smem capacity

## Key Lessons

- XOR-based swizzle permutation eliminates the need for shared-memory padding in GEMM B tiles — it reclaims the ~50% smem overhead that padding wastes while achieving equivalent bank-conflict avoidance through address remapping
- At small problem sizes (M=N=K=256, only 2×2 blocks), shared-memory bank conflicts have inherently low impact due to few concurrent warps per SM, so both padding and swizzle show near-identical performance — the benefit of swizzle (smem savings) becomes significant at larger tile counts
- Swizzle-based approaches trade a small amount of address-computation logic for substantial shared-memory capacity savings, which improves occupancy when multiple K-stages or larger tile sizes are needed

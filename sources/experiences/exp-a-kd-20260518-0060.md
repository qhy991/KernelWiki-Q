## Key Lessons

- Use compile-time scale-type enums (e.g., NoBetaScaling) to let the compiler eliminate dead beta multiplies in epilogue fusion rather than relying on runtime beta values — turns a full fragment multiply loop into a register copy
- Microbenchmark results for instruction-level optimizations may appear neutral when kernel launch overhead and memory traffic dominate at small problem sizes — validate on workloads where arithmetic intensity is high enough to expose the savings
- nvcc at -O3 may partially optimize a beta=1.0 multiply into a register copy, further eroding measured gains — explicit specialization via templates or compile-time flags is still preferred for clarity and guaranteed elimination

## Solution

Swap grid dimensions from <<<M, N>>> to <<<N, M>>> and reverse the indexing formula from (row + col*ld) to (col + row*ld), so consecutive warp threads access column-major data at stride 1 instead of stride LDC, converting non-coalesced scattered loads into fully coalesced contiguous loads

## Key Lessons

- In column-major storage layouts, ensure threadIdx.x maps to the row dimension (contiguous in memory) rather than the column dimension to achieve stride-1 coalesced warp access — applies to all kernels reading from column-major workspaces or matrices
- Swapping grid dimensions (<<<M,N>>> to <<<N,M>>>) combined with reversing the row/col indexing formula preserves computational correctness while converting stride-LDC scattered access into stride-1 coalesced access

## Solution

Replace the branch-based conditional swap with a branchless TOPK_SWAP macro that uses min/max intrinsics to unconditionally compute both orderings and assign them, eliminating the explicit branch and relying on predication-friendly instructions

## Key Lessons

- Branchless min/max swap vs branch-based conditional swap yield identical predicated code for N=2 sorting — the CUDA compiler already converts simple if-swap patterns to predicated moves, making the optimization stylistic rather than performance-relevant at small N
- Branchless sorting patterns become worthwhile at larger N (N>=8) where the compiler may not fully eliminate divergent branches; for N=2 and N=4 the if-swap is already predicated and the manual min/max approach adds no measurable benefit
- Template-based Sort2 with a TOPK_SWAP macro provides cleaner extensibility for larger sorting networks even when the performance impact is neutral at N=2

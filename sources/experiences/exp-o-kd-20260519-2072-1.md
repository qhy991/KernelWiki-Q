## Challenge

CUTLASS int8 GEMM with TileShape<128,128,64> on M=64 problem wastes half the threadblock's M-direction capacity — tile M=128 covers only 64 valid rows, leaving 2 of 4 warps producing masked-out data and only 50% M-tile utilization


## Solution

Match tile M dimension exactly to problem M (TileShape<64,128,128> for M=64), eliminating the wasted second warp row in M while deepening K from 64 to 128 stages to improve pipeline utilization and shared-memory reuse within the same threadblock budget

## Key Lessons

- Match CUTLASS TileShape M dimension to the actual problem M when M is small — oversized tile M wastes warps on out-of-bounds computation and reduces effective occupancy
- When shrinking tile M to match the problem, the freed threadblock budget can be reinvested into tile K depth (64→128), which deepens the software pipeline and increases shared-memory reuse per stage
- TileShape selection should be problem-size-aware: a tile that is optimal for large M may be severely suboptimal for small M even within the same kernel family

## Solution

Split the single evictable category into two sub-categories — misses (recently loaded from host) and stale evictables — using a three-branch write-back that places misses near the MRU end (right before hits) and only stale slots at the LRU front, protecting freshly fetched tokens from immediate re-eviction

## Key Lessons

- Algorithmic correctness fixes that add one branch per iteration can be performance-neutral at the single-kernel level but deliver system-level gains by eliminating redundant host-to-device transfers across multiple inference steps
- In LRU cache write-back, freshly loaded miss entries must be placed near the MRU end (not the LRU front) to prevent thrashing — the recency of loading should confer temporary protection from eviction
- Microbenchmarks of tiny kernels (1 block, ~2.5us) are dominated by launch overhead; a ~1% latency difference is noise, not a meaningful performance signal

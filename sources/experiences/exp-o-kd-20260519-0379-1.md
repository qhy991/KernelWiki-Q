## Challenge

FlashAttention MMA kernel stores the O accumulator in FP16 registers (2 uint32_t per element, kOStorageAccFloat32=0), forcing repeated half-to-float and float-to-half conversions (__half2float / __float2half_rn) at every online softmax rescale step across K/V tiles, wasting conversion instructions and losing ~3-4 decimal places of precision per rescale iteration


## Solution

Switch O accumulator storage to FP32 registers (4 uint32_t per element via float* reinterpretation, kOStorageAccFloat32=1), enabling the online softmax rescale FMA to operate entirely in float without any half conversion instructions until the final write-back, improving both throughput (eliminated conversion overhead) and numerical precision across all rescale iterations

## Key Lessons

- Storing FlashAttention O accumulators in FP32 instead of FP16 registers eliminates per-tile half-to-float-to-half conversion overhead and preserves numerical precision through all online softmax rescale iterations — applies whenever the kernel performs iterative rescaling and register pressure allows the 2x storage increase
- FP32 accumulator storage enables the compiler to generate more efficient FMA chains for rescaling because both accumulator and rescale factor are natively float, avoiding intermediate conversion instructions that break FMA fusion
- The register cost tradeoff (4 uint32_t vs 2 uint32_t per O element) is worthwhile when the number of K/V tiles is large enough that the eliminated conversion overhead exceeds the potential register spilling cost — in this benchmark with SEQ_LEN=512, FP32 storage yields 1.103x speedup

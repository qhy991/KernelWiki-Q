## Solution

Replace per-element atomicAdd with register-local accumulation using a thread-private counter, then issue a single atomicAdd per thread after the loop completes, reducing atomic operations from O(topk_length) to at most THREADS_PER_EXPERT=512 per block and eliminating inter-thread serialization on the shared counter

## Key Lessons

- Accumulate per-thread partial counts in a register variable and issue a single atomicAdd after the loop instead of atomicAdd on every iteration — eliminates O(N) atomic operations that serialize on a shared address, replacing them with O(blockDim) atomics with negligible contention
- Branchless predicate-to-int conversion `(condition)` is both shorter and avoids warp divergence compared to an if-guard around atomicAdd, since the addend is zero for non-matching elements and the atomic is already outside the loop
